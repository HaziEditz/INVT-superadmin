BookaWaka Towing — Full Platform Flow

Towing company finds BookaWaka:

Sees it on bookawaka.co.nz → clicks "Join as a Towing Operator"
Fills in: company name, contact name, email, phone, city, fleet size, truck types, operating hours, after-hours yes/no
Selects service type: Towing
Submits → lands in SA portal as a pending registration
SA Admin (you) reviews it:

See it in SA-Onboard.aspx
Approve → system auto-creates their Company ID + login credentials
They get a welcome email with their Owner Portal login link
2. OWNER PANEL SETUP
Towing company logs into their Owner Portal. First time they set everything up:

Company Profile:

Logo, trading name, address, phone, email
Operating hours (Mon–Sun, open/close times)
After-hours toggle — yes/no
On-call driver for after hours (they nominate which driver)
Fleet Setup:

Add each truck: name/plate, type (flatbed / wheel-lift / heavy recovery / multi-car), capacity (how many vehicles it can carry at once), year, photo
Mark as active/inactive
Pricing Setup:

Base callout fee (e.g. $95)
Per km rate (e.g. $3.50/km)
Surcharges: motorcycle (+$20), SUV (+$30), heavy vehicle (+$80)
After-hours surcharge (e.g. +$50)
Public holiday surcharge
All shown to customer as a price estimate before they book
Service Area:

Set the areas/suburbs they cover
Maximum job distance they'll accept
Payment Settings:

Stripe connected (BookaWaka takes commission %)
Insurance jobs toggle — yes they accept insurance
Cash jobs toggle
Drivers:

Add drivers: name, phone, licence, truck assigned
Each driver gets app login credentials
Notifications:

Email + SMS for new jobs
Who gets notified (dispatcher email, on-call driver phone)
3. CUSTOMER BOOKS A TOW
Via Passenger App or Website:

Customer taps "Request a Tow" (or selects Towing on website)
Enters:
Current location (GPS or typed)
Vehicle: make, model, year, colour, registration
Problem: broke down / accident / flat tyre / locked out / other
Drop-off: home address / nearest garage / towing yard / other
Payment: Insurance (enter company + policy number) or Pay now via Stripe
Optional: schedule for later or right now
System shows: estimated price, estimated wait time, towing company name + rating
Customer confirms → booking created
Payment happens here:

If paying by Stripe → charged immediately, truck dispatched
If insurance → no charge, towing company handles insurer invoice separately
4. DISPATCH
During business hours:

Job appears in towing company's dispatch portal instantly
Dispatcher sees: customer name, phone, location on map, vehicle details, drop-off, payment type
Dispatcher picks a truck (system shows which trucks are free vs on a job)
Assigns → driver gets notification on their app immediately
Dispatcher can call customer if needed
After hours (no dispatcher):

Job goes directly to on-call driver's app as a push notification + SMS
Driver has 5 minutes to accept
If accepted → job begins, customer sees live tracking
If no response → customer gets notification: "Driver will call you shortly" + driver's direct number shown
No trucks available:

Customer sees: "All trucks are currently busy — estimated wait X mins" OR "No availability right now"
Option: Leave your number, we'll call you
Dispatcher calls customer manually with update

Customer name + phone number
Pickup location (map + address)
Vehicle: make, model, colour, rego
Problem description
Drop-off address
Payment: Paid via Stripe / Insurance (don't collect) / Cash (collect $X)
Any notes from dispatcher
Driver actions:

Accept job

Driver sees only towing jobs — not taxi, food, freight.

6. PASSENGER EXPERIENCE DURING THE JOB
After booking confirmed:

Live map showing truck location + ETA (updates every 30 seconds)
Status bar: Assigned → En Route → Arrived → Loading → Completed
Driver name + photo + truck plate shown
One-tap call to driver
Estimated final price shown (updates if extra charges added)
When job is completed:

Receipt sent via email + in-app
Rating prompt (rate the driver/service)
Cross-sell appears immediately (see section 7)
7. TAXI CROSS-SELL (THE CLEVER BIT)
This is the same Ride-to-Rental idea applied to towing.

After the tow is confirmed (truck is coming), the customer is stranded. They need to get somewhere. Immediately in the app:

"Need a ride while your car is being towed?"

BookaWaka automatically books them a taxi from their current location
Destination: wherever they want to go (home, work, garage, wherever the car is going)
They get a discount code — e.g. 15% off their taxi ride because they're a towing customer
One tap → taxi is booked
This means:

Customer doesn't wait stranded — they go with the taxi while the truck handles the car
BookaWaka earns the towing commission AND the taxi fare
Taxi company gets a guaranteed booking
Everyone wins
The promo code flow is already built (same as Ride-to-Rental for rentals). This just reuses that system with a different trigger.

8. JOB COMPLETE — MONEY FLOW
Stripe payment → BookaWaka collects full amount → pays towing company (minus commission %) at payout cycle
Insurance job → towing company invoices insurer directly → BookaWaka records the job for reporting but doesn't process money
Cash job → driver collects cash → towing company reports it → BookaWaka records for commission invoicing
9. SA PORTAL SIDE (what you manage)
SA-Towing.aspx — all towing companies, all jobs, revenue, commission

All jobs table: filterable by company, status, payment type, date
Payouts: what BookaWaka owes each towing company
Taxi cross-sell stats: how many taxi rides were generated from towing jobs

Planning insurance commission strategy (13 seconds)
Really good questions — and these are genuinely tricky. Let me be straight with you on both:

Question 1: Commission on insurance jobs

The problem is exactly what you said — you don't know what the insurer pays the towing company. They deal directly. You can't take a % of something you can't see.

Solution: Flat fee per insurance job instead of %

Instead of commission on the job value, BookaWaka charges the towing company a flat fee per insurance job processed through the platform. For example:

Stripe job (customer pays) → BookaWaka takes 12% commission as normal
Insurance job → BookaWaka charges towing company a flat $10–15 admin/referral fee per job
The towing company self-reports the job was completed. BookaWaka invoices them monthly for all insurance jobs processed. Simple, fair, and you're not relying on knowing what the insurer paid.

This also means towing companies are happy — they're not giving away % of a big insurance payout, just a small flat fee.

Question 2: Accident — Driver B's insurance should pay for Driver A's tow

This is a third-party claim situation and honestly, the platform cannot chase Driver B's insurer — that's a legal matter between the drivers. Here's how to handle it practically:

On the passenger app when Driver A books:

They see three payment options:

My insurance — enter your own insurer + policy
Third party insurance — enter the other driver's insurer + policy (if they gave it)
Pay now, claim later — pay via Stripe upfront, then claim it back from Driver B's insurance yourself
If Driver B didn't give insurance details:
The app shows: "If you don't have the other driver's insurance details, we recommend paying now and claiming reimbursement later through your own insurer or police report."

Driver A pays via Stripe. They get a receipt with all job details — time, location, vehicle, km, cost — which they use to make their insurance claim.

On the driver app (tow truck driver on scene):

Driver can record at scene:

Photo of Driver A's vehicle (damage)
Photo of Driver B's vehicle (if still there)
Driver B's plate number
Driver B's insurance company + policy if given
Any police report number
This creates a documented record in the system. The towing company can use it when dealing with insurers. BookaWaka stores it against the job record.

The bottom line:

Situation	Platform handles	Towing company handles
Driver A has own insurance	Records insurer details, connects them	Bills insurer directly
Driver B paying for Driver A	Records details if available, Driver A pays Stripe	Driver A claims back
Driver B gives no details	Driver A pays Stripe, gets full receipt for claim	Nothing extra
Insurance amount unknown	Flat fee per job, not %	Keeps full insurance payment
