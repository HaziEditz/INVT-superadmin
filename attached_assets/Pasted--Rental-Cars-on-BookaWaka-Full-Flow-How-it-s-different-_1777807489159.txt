🚗 Rental Cars on BookaWaka — Full Flow
How it's different from taxi
Taxi	Rental
Driver	Assigned by platform	Customer drives themselves
Duration	Trip (minutes)	Booking (hours/days/weeks)
Pricing	Distance + time	Rate × duration
Payment	On completion	Upfront + deposit
Vehicle	Platform's problem	Rental company's fleet
Availability	Real-time drivers	Calendar-based
Two models — which one you should offer
Model A — Self-pickup (Simpler)
Customer books online → goes to rental company depot → picks up car → returns it.
BookaWaka handles booking + payment. Rental company handles everything physical.

Model B — Vehicle Delivery (Premium)
Customer books → rental company driver delivers the car to the customer → customer drives it → driver collects it when done.
This uses your existing driver app — same dispatch flow, source = rental.

Start with Model A. Add Model B later when rental companies ask for it.

Customer Booking Flow
1. Open passenger app or website
2. Tap "Rent a Car"
3. Enter: pickup date + time, return date + time, pickup location
4. Browse available vehicles (filtered by availability + location)
5. Tap a vehicle → see full details (photos, specs, what's included)
6. See price breakdown:
      Daily rate × days
      + Add-ons selected
      + Platform fee
      + Refundable deposit
      = Total
7. Pay:  Stripe charges full rental + holds deposit as authorisation
8. Booking confirmed → Job ID generated (source = rental)
9. Customer gets: confirmation, pickup address, rental company contact
10. Customer picks up car at depot (shows booking ID)
11. On return → rental company confirms return in Owner Panel
12. Deposit released automatically via Stripe (or partially kept for damage)

What Happens with the Deposit
This is the tricky part — Stripe handles it cleanly:

At booking:    Stripe authorises (holds) the deposit — money not taken yet
At pickup:     Rental company confirms pickup in Owner Panel → deposit capture confirmed
At return:     Rental company marks returned → deposit released (or damage amount kept)
If damage:     Owner Panel → enter damage amount → Stripe captures that amount, releases rest

This is called a Stripe Payment Intent with manual capture — it's already possible with your Stripe setup.

Planning rental car pricing flow (6 seconds)
This is a serious pricing engine. Here's the complete flow covering all 8 areas:

🚗 BookaWaka Rental — Complete Pricing Engine Flow
1. BASE CHARGES — Time Billing
How rental companies set it:

Per vehicle, owner panel sets:
  Hourly rate:  $15/hr  (for short rentals, e.g. under 24hrs)
  Daily rate:   $80/day
  Weekly rate:  $420/week (usually discounted vs daily × 7)
  Monthly rate: $1,400/mo (optional)

The 25-hour rule — how time rounds up:

0–1 hr     → 1 hr charge (or minimum 3hrs)
1–24 hrs   → 1 day charge
25 hrs     → 2 days  ← your example — rounds to full next day
48 hrs     → 2 days
49 hrs     → 3 days
Rule: ceil(total hours / 24) = days billed
If hourly rate is offered: first 24hrs charged at daily rate, 
  each hour after = hourly rate (up to next day threshold)

Weekly/monthly breakpoints:

If rental ≥ 7 days → weekly rate kicks in automatically (cheaper)
If rental ≥ 28 days → monthly rate kicks in
System shows customer: "Tip: adding 1 more day drops to weekly rate and saves $X"

2. VEHICLE CATEGORY PRICING
Categories owner sets (each with own price):

Category	Example daily rate	Who uses it
Economy	$45/day	Budget travellers, city use
Compact	$55/day	Small families
Standard/Mid-size	$70/day	Most common
SUV / 4WD	$95/day	Families, outdoors, NZ rural
People Mover / Van	$110/day	Groups, sports teams
Luxury	$180/day	Business, special occasions
Ute/Truck	$120/day	Tradespeople, moving
Per-vehicle overrides:
Even within a category, each specific car can have its own price. A brand new SUV costs more than an older one in the same category. Owner sets category default + per-vehicle adjustment.

Displayed to customer as:

Economy from $45/day  → click to see specific cars available
SUV from $89/day      → click to see specific cars available

The "from" price is the advertised anchor (Point 7 — advertising).

3. EXTRA FEES (Add-ons)
Owner panel sets which add-ons they offer and the price:

Always-available extras:
Additional Driver       $X/day or $X flat per rental
Young Driver (under 25) $X/day surcharge  ← system detects DOB from booking
GPS / Satnav            $X/day
Child Seat (infant)     $X/day
Booster Seat            $X/day
Roof Rack               $X flat
Snow Chains             $X flat (seasonal)

Fuel charges:
Policy options (rental company picks one):
  A. Full-to-Full: Customer returns with full tank. If not:
       Fuel shortfall fee = missing litres × pump rate + $X admin fee
  B. Pre-purchase fuel: Customer pays for a full tank upfront.
       No refund for unused fuel. Simple.
  C. Fuel Inclusive: Price already includes fuel (owner sets this)

Fuel shortfall at return → Owner Panel enters litres missing → Stripe captures from deposit

How add-ons show in booking:
Step 1: Choose car
Step 2: Choose extras (each toggleable, price shown per day × rental days)
Step 3: Price summary updates live as they add/remove
Step 4: Confirm + pay

4. LOCATION-BASED PRICING
Rental company sets pickup locations, each with a location surcharge:

Locations owner sets:
  Airport (AKL)       + $35 flat surcharge  ← airport concession fee passed on
  City Branch (CBD)   + $0   (base rate)
  Suburb Branch       - $10  (cheaper, incentivise)
  Hotel delivery      + $25 delivery fee
  One-way (drop at different location) + $X one-way fee

One-way rentals:
Customer picks up at Airport, drops off at City. Owner sets the one-way fee. System shows it clearly before booking.

Location surcharge logic:

Base daily rate: $80
Airport pickup:  $80 + $35/rental (not per day — flat fee)
Customer sees:  "$80/day | Airport fee: $35 (one-time)"

5. MILEAGE / DISTANCE
Owner sets per vehicle or per category:

Option A — Unlimited km:
  No tracking. Price includes all km.
  "Unlimited mileage included" badge shown.
Option B — Daily km limit:
  e.g. 150 km/day included
  Extra km: $0.25/km (owner sets the rate)
  System calculates: rental days × 150 = total included km
  Customer told upfront: "150 km/day included, $0.25/km after"
Option C — Total km limit:
  e.g. 500 km total for the rental
  Extra: $0.28/km
Miles vs km: Owner sets which unit. System converts for display.

How extra km is charged:

On return: rental company enters odometer reading in Owner Panel
System calculates: km used - km included = extra km
Extra charge = extra km × rate per km
Stripe captures this amount from deposit (or charges separately)

6. TIME-BASED PENALTIES
Late Return:
Grace period: 30–59 minutes → no charge (owner sets grace)
60–3 hrs late  → hourly rate charged per hour over
3+ hrs late    → full extra day charged
Owner panel sets:
  Grace period (mins): 30
  Late fee hourly rate: $20/hr
  Full day threshold: 3hrs

Early Return:
Options (owner chooses per rental):
  A. No refund for unused days (most rental companies)
  B. Partial refund (credit for unused full days, not partial days)
  C. Full refund (unlikely but owner can offer)
  
Owner panel default setting + customer sees policy at booking:
"Early returns: no refund for unused days"

No-show / Cancellation:
Owner sets cancellation policy:
  Free cancellation: up to X hours before pickup
  Late cancellation (within X hrs): $X fee or % of rental
  No-show: full first day charge
  
Stripe handles: if cancellation is chargeable, partial capture from deposit
or new charge against saved card

8. INSURANCE PACKAGES
This is the most important upsell in car rental — most margin is made here.

How it works — 3 tiers:
Tier 1 — Basic Cover (always included, no extra charge):

What it covers:  Third party liability (legally required)
Excess (what customer pays if damage):  $2,500–$4,000
Theft excess:    $2,500
Windscreen:      NOT covered (customer pays full replacement)
Undercarriage:   NOT covered
Tyres:           NOT covered

Tier 2 — Standard Protection (mid-price add-on):

Name on platform: "Standard Protection"
Price example: +$18/day
Reduces excess to: $800
Covers: windscreen (1 per rental), tyres
