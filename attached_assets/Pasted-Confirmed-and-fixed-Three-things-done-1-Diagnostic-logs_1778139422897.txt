Confirmed — and fixed. Three things done:

[Jobs] onValue fired — exists: … | status: … | bookingId: … now fires at the very top of the jobs-path callback.

These will show immediately in the driver app console the moment Firebase delivers anything to either path — even if the app was backgrounded and reconnected. If neither log appears after dispatch writes the offer, the WebSocket has dropped and the listeners have detached.

2. Root cause found and fixed — the accept modal was never shown from the jobs path

The onValue listener on jobs/{cid}/{vehicleId}/{driverId} was never wired to show the accept/decline modal. When a brand-new offer arrived on that path and the driver was free, the code jumped directly to status: 'current' — skipping setIncomingJob entirely. No popup. The driver was already "on a job" they never agreed to.

Fixed: brand-new offer + free driver now:

Creates the job as status: 'offered'
Calls setIncomingJob(...) → accept/decline modal appears
Fires a local push notification if the app is backgrounded
Writes allbookings/{cid}/{bookingId}/Status: 'Offered' so your console reflects offer delivery
This mirrors exactly what the notification-path listener already did. Driver-busy path is unchanged (silent offer, badge only, no popup).
